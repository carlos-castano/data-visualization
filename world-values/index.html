How Values are Distributed Worldwide?

Thanks to surveys conducted by the World Values Survey organization between 2016 and 2022 (using the most updated), we can ask ourselves how values are distributed in the world through 2 variables scaled on a range -1, 1.

  • Traditional - Secular: low values in this index represent a high importance of religion, parent-child bonds, deference to authority and traditional family values, as well as the rejection of divorce, abortion, euthanasia, suicide and national perspective. While high values indicate the opposite preferences.
  • Survival - Self-expression: low values in this index emphasize economic and physical security, with a relatively ethnocentric perspective and low levels of trust and tolerance. High values indicate a high priority for environmental protection, growing tolerance of foreigners, gays and lesbians, and gender equality, as well as increasing demands for participation in decision-making in economic and political life.

All population data used are from 2022 regardless of when the survey was conducted.
For the sake of simplicity, the total population is considered to be those countries surveyed (5.24 billion people).
